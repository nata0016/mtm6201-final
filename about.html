“What is the good life?”

Over the last 6 years (whether I knew it or not) I pursued this question. I failed, I prospered, I took really big risks, I fought, laughed, cried and found that the answer didn’t lie in any destination... but in the process of growing, learning, and finding joy in life's quiet moments.
I suppose that's why I'm here now. This really happy accident of becoming a photographer emerged from fiercely chasing after “the good life” and finding it right in front of me – in the people I meet, the stories I’m welcomed into, and the moments witnessed and experienced. This is the heart behind what I do. To create images that show it all – our humanness in split seconds.
